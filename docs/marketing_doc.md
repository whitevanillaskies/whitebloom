# Marketing

Raw ideas, copy directions, and pitch sketches. Nothing here is final.


## Core positioning

**Desktop for your desktop.**

Enter through a modest door. Let the depth reveal itself.

The cute door is the whiteboard — spatial, tactile, immediately familiar. The depth is everything underneath: files on disk, an open module system, agents that traverse it as peers. Most tools in this space invert this — the pitch is grandiose, the product is a notes app. Here the pitch is almost casual and the product keeps surprising you long after you're in.

The emotional arc a user should have: *oh, a whiteboard* → *wait, these nodes open into things* → *wait, it's just files?* → *wait, I can add my own module?* Each layer recontextualizes the one before it. That's the product doing the marketing.


## The messy desktop pitch

A sketch for a landing page or short video.

---

We heard you like desktops.

You like them so much that you really want to use them —

but you only have one desktop.

So they end up looking like this.

*[image: catastrophically messy desktop — icons everywhere]*

Or you hate them looking like that, so you barely use your desktop at all.

*[image: completely empty desktop]*

So we made a desktop for your desktop.

Now you can have as many as you want.

*[a node opens into something richer than expected — no explanation, just the moment]*

---

**Notes on the pitch:**

- The messy/empty split is the hook. It's universal and it frames both behaviors as the same problem — you either live in the mess or you avoid the tool entirely. The product solves both.
- "We heard you like desktops" is wry without being smug. It earns the pitch before it sells anything. Don't over-polish it.
- Cut "Name them. Share them. Organize them." — generic, slows the momentum. Go straight to the reveal.
- The reveal should be a single wordless moment: a node opens, something unexpected is inside. No voiceover, no label. The viewer completes the thought themselves, which is more persuasive than being told.
- Never explain "bloom" in marketing. If the word appears at all, it appears in the product UI and earns its meaning there. On a landing page it sounds like a feature name. Let the action speak.


## Vocabulary

| Word | Where it lives |
|---|---|
| Desktop for your desktop | Hero headline, first impression |
| Bloom / blooms | Inside the product only — earns meaning through use, not explanation |
| OS for knowledge | Long-form: blog posts, talks, deep-dives |
| Cute door | Internal shorthand for the design philosophy — never in copy |
